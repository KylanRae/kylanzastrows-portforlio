Who am I?

My name is Kylan Zastrow, a trans-masculine non-binary person. I am mainly a graphic designer and a website developper. But that isn't everything. I am also an up and coming makeup artist and a musician.

My Graphic Design Journey

I have been designing since I was 12 years old and my passion has just continued to grow as I grew into the person I am suppose to be. After graduating high school, I went to the Graphic Communications program at Saskatchewan Polytechnic, where I went, not just to learn how to be a professional designer, but to grow my own style in design.

My Queer Journey

I had to spend years to come to terms with who am I and that I am LGBTQ+. I went on a journey that started with accepting that it's okay for me to be gay. It took me far too long, but with the toxic queer people surrounding me, it was hard to feel that I would fit in and that I wanted to be queer. It was only the summer before grade 12 that I had come to terms with myself. I am an Agender, trans-masculine, polysexual. It was a scary journey, but this is where my activism lies.
